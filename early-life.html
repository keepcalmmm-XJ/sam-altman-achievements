Introduction

Samuel Harris Altman was born on April 22, 1985, in Chicago, Illinois. He spent most of his childhood growing up in St. Louis, Missouri, where his early experiences would shape his future as a technology entrepreneur and visionary.

From an early age, Altman displayed remarkable curiosity and intelligence that would eventually lead him to become one of the most influential figures in the tech industry. His journey from a curious child in the Midwest to a Silicon Valley powerhouse is a testament to his unique vision and determination.

Early Interest in Technology

1993 (Age 8)

Sam received his first computer, an Apple Macintosh, which sparked his lifelong passion for technology. This early exposure to computing would prove pivotal in shaping his future career path.

Early Childhood

Beyond just using his computer, young Sam developed a fascination with understanding how technology worked. He began disassembling computer hardware and teaching himself to code, demonstrating the curiosity and technical aptitude that would later define his career.

High School Years

Sam Altman attended John Burroughs School, a prestigious private school in St. Louis, Missouri. During his time there, he not only excelled academically but also demonstrated remarkable personal courage and leadership.

"I came out as gay when I was 17 years old. It was a different time then, and I wanted to make the environment better for other students like me."

At the age of 17, Altman came out as gay to his peers and teachers. This was a significant moment not just in his personal life but also in his development as a leader. He became an advocate for creating a more inclusive environment for gay students at his school, showing early signs of the leadership qualities that would later define his professional career.

His time at John Burroughs School helped shape his worldview and provided him with a strong educational foundation that would prepare him for his future endeavors in technology and entrepreneurship.

University Education

In 2003, Sam Altman enrolled at Stanford University to study computer science. Stanford, located in the heart of Silicon Valley, is renowned for its strong computer science program and connections to the tech industry.

However, after just two years of study, Altman made the bold decision to drop out in 2005. This decision, while unconventional, aligned with his entrepreneurial spirit and desire to learn through real-world experience rather than traditional academic settings.

Interestingly, Altman has often stated that he learned more valuable skills from playing poker during his university years than from his formal lectures. The game taught him crucial skills in:

  • ✓ Pattern recognition
  • ✓ Decision-making under uncertainty
  • ✓ Risk assessment
  • ✓ Reading people

These skills would prove invaluable in his future career as an entrepreneur and investor, where making decisions with incomplete information is a daily necessity.

The Stanford Decision

Altman's decision to leave Stanford after two years reflects his belief in practical learning and real-world experience over traditional education paths.
